Membership Benefits
For organizations:
Our partners are essential collaborators, enriching our journey with their expertise and support.

You become part of the world’s largest business organization, whose members include some of the most influential and dynamic companies, as well as leading experts in international trade and dispute resolution.

You benefit from ICC’s privileged links with major intergovernmental organizations, including the United Nations, the World Trade Organizations, and the G20/B20.

You contribute to advocating business positions that are brought to the attention of the national government.

You help write the rules and standards that business uses every day to reduce costs and uncertainties in world trade, such as the Incoterms Rules, the Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits (UCP), and the ICC Model Contracts.

You stay informed of the latest legislative, technological, and regulatory developments in international business through ICC’s 12 global commissions.

You become eligible to participate in any of the ICC’s Commissions, as well as local Commissions, influencing the rules and guidelines issued by ICC and collaborating with a unique international group of peers in your field of interest, as well as national legislations reflecting the interest of the Qatari private sector.

You receive invitations to in-person and online events, meetings, webinars, and more, those at the international and regional level, such as the World Chambers Congress. Members are eligible to a discounted rate for paid events.

You receive a complimentary subscription to the ICC Dispute Resolution Bulletin, featuring commentary, extracts and analysis of case decisions, updates and guidance on ICC dispute resolution processes, book reviews and more.

You build skills and extend your network by receiving member discounts on:

Exclusive ICC events on international trade, banking and dispute resolution.

ICC Academy online courses and certifications on trade finance, export/import, and ethical marketing and advertising.

ICC Publications, covering vital areas of international business such as arbitration, trade finance, commercial contracts and anti-corruption.

For Chambers of Commerce:

You automatically become members of the ICC World Chambers Federation (WCF), the backbone of the global chamber community, that unites the network of local, regional, national, and bilateral chambers. A unique and global platform, WCF not only helps chambers innovate, but unites them on common issues at the grassroots level in their respective communities and within their organizations.

As the local voice of business, your chamber is a key ally and point of contact in your country, helping us to ensure that ICC positions and policy priorities reach your local authorities, and that essential ICC tools and services are disseminated at your local business community.

National Policy Commissions

The International Court of Arbitration is the world’s leading arbitral institution. Since 1923, ICC has been helping to resolve disputes in international commercial and investment disputes.

We perform an essential role by providing individuals, businesses and governments alike with a variety of customizable services for every stage of their dispute.

Although we are called a court in name, we do not make formal judgments on disputed matters. Instead, we exercise judicial supervision of arbitration proceedings. Our responsibilities include:

• Confirming, appointing and replacing arbitrators, as well as deciding on any challenges made against them

• Monitoring the arbitral process to make certain that it is performed properly and with the required speed and efficiency necessary

• Scrutinising and approving all arbitral awards to reinforce quality and enforceability

• Setting, managing and — if necessary — adjusting fees and advances

• Overseeing emergency proceedings before the start of the arbitration

Our purpose is to ensure proper application of the ICC rules, as well as assist parties and arbitrators in overcoming procedural obstacles. These efforts are supported by the court’s secretariat, which is made up of more than 80 lawyers and support

ICC Court Members: Appointments Overview - Local Appointments

Members of the ICC Court are appointed for three-year terms by the ICC World Council. This is done on the proposal of one member from ICC national committees and other ICC groups, or upon proposal by the President of the Court.

Once appointed, ICC Court members must remain independent from national committees in the performance of their functions. Alternate members are appointed by the World Council on the proposal of the ICC Court’s President.
